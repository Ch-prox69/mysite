What Kind of Music do I Play on the Drums?

I play to classic rock, heavy metal, and a bit of blues if you count Led Zeppelin being blues. Some of my favorite bands to play to are Led Zeppelin, The Who, Kansas, Deep Purple, Toto, Judas Priest, and Queen. My favorite drummer is John Bonham from Led Zeppelin. He's widely considered to be one of the greatest drummers of all time! My favorite songs to play to are Led Zeppelin - Custard Pie, The Who - Won't Get Fooled Again, Nazareth - Hair of the Dog, and Led Zeppelin - D'yer Mak'er.

Are the Drums a Hard Instrument to Play?

It depends. To most people, the drums just look like some random guy flailing his arms around hitting random drums that make noise. This is just plain wrong. The drums are a backbone of a band because it gives the band that beat that it needs to make the music pop. The Drums require technique from holding the sticks to learning what sounds right with what. Now, with all this, the drums aren't as hard as an instrument like a pipe organ or a harp, but still a hard instrument regardless. Now to actually play, you don't need to know sheet music, at least in my opinion you don't. Most rock drummers rarely ever touched sheet music because the drums are about feeling what's right by sound, so most rock drummers usually don't make sheet music for their songs. The hardest part about playing the drums isn't getting the right notes or making sure everything is perfect, it's endurance. Most drummers should be able to play for at least an hour. Drumming is also a workout too. You play at high speeds and generate a lot of movement, so this causes most drummers to sweat like crazy.

What are some Drawbacks to plating the Drums?

Some drawbacks of playing the drums are hearing loss, blisters, and carpel tunnel syndrome. For hearing loss, this is caused by a culmination of thousands of hours of playing drums, acoustic set, without hearing protection. Most professional drummers, Phil Collins and Dave Grohl to name a few, have to wear hearing aids due to loss of hearing. The best way to protect your hearing is using earbuds, but even then you will still likely get hearing loss, so there's really no way of working around it unless you give up playing all together, which no one will do that. Another problem is blisters. This is an issue if you play very fast and long, which is just asking for blisters. The best way to prevent blisters are either using hand lotion, or wearing gloves. I've tried both, and lotion is the best, although it makes it a bit tricky to hold the sticks in place. The last issue you might run into is carpel tunnel syndrome. While there aren't a lot of pro drummers who have reported having CTS, it's still a possibility. To mitigate this, make sure to strech out your hands to mitigate the risk.
